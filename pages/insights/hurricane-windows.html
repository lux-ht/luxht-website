Choosing Hurricane-Resistant Windows & Doors

Updated January 2026 • 9 min read

In Florida, your windows and doors are your home's most vulnerable points during a hurricane. Understanding your options for protection can save your home—and significantly reduce your insurance costs.

Why Window Protection Matters

When a window or door fails during a hurricane:

  • Wind enters the home, pressurizing the interior
  • Roof can lift off from internal pressure
  • Flying debris causes additional damage
  • Rain destroys interior contents

Protecting openings is the single most important thing you can do to protect your home from hurricane damage.

Impact Windows: The Gold Standard

How They Work

Impact-resistant windows use laminated glass with a plastic interlayer (similar to car windshields). When struck, the glass may crack but stays in place, maintaining the building envelope.

Benefits

  • 24/7 protection: No preparation needed before storms
  • Noise reduction: 25-50% quieter than standard windows
  • UV protection: Blocks 99% of harmful UV rays
  • Security: Difficult to break through (deters burglars)
  • Energy efficiency: Most include Low-E coating
  • Insurance savings: 15-45% reduction in premiums

Cost

  • Average cost: $40-80 per square foot installed
  • Typical home (15-20 windows): $15,000-35,000
  • Payback through insurance savings: 7-15 years

Florida Building Code Requirement

Since 2002, all new construction in Florida's High-Velocity Hurricane Zone must have impact-resistant windows or approved shutters. Even outside this zone, impact protection is highly recommended.

Hurricane Shutters: Types and Costs

Accordion Shutters

  • Permanently mounted, fold closed when needed
  • Quick deployment (minutes)
  • Can be locked from inside
  • Cost: $15-25 per square foot
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years

Roll-Down Shutters

  • Most convenient option (besides impact windows)
  • Manual or electric operation
  • Can be operated from inside
  • Cost: $25-50 per square foot
  • Lifespan: 20+ years

Colonial/Bahama Shutters

  • Decorative appearance
  • Bahama: hinged at top, provide shade
  • Colonial: hinged at sides, traditional look
  • Cost: $20-35 per square foot
  • Require going outside to close

Storm Panels

  • Most affordable option
  • Aluminum or steel panels
  • Require storage space
  • More time-consuming to install
  • Cost: $7-15 per square foot

Plywood

  • Budget emergency option
  • Must be 5/8" or thicker CDX plywood
  • Pre-cut and label before hurricane season
  • Not as effective as rated products
  • May not qualify for insurance discounts

Impact Doors

Entry Doors

  • Impact-rated steel or fiberglass
  • Reinforced frame and hardware
  • Multi-point locking systems
  • Cost: $2,000-5,000 installed

Sliding Glass Doors

  • Often the largest opening in the home
  • Impact sliding doors available
  • Require shutters if not impact-rated
  • Cost: $3,000-8,000 installed

Garage Doors

  • Critical weak point in many homes
  • Hurricane-rated doors available
  • Reinforcement kits for existing doors
  • Cost: $1,000-3,000 for reinforcement; $2,000-5,000 for replacement

Insurance Savings and Wind Mitigation

Wind Mitigation Inspection

A wind mitigation inspection documents hurricane-resistant features:

  • Cost: $75-150
  • Valid for 5 years
  • Documents roof, openings, and connections
  • Can reduce insurance 15-45%+

Features That Reduce Premiums

  • Opening protection (impact windows/shutters)
  • Roof construction and age
  • Hurricane straps/clips
  • Secondary water resistance

Making the Decision: Windows vs. Shutters

Choose Impact Windows If:

  • You want zero storm preparation
  • You're replacing windows anyway
  • You value noise reduction and security
  • You plan to stay in the home long-term
  • Budget allows for higher upfront cost

Choose Shutters If:

  • Budget is limited
  • Windows are newer and in good condition
  • You're comfortable with pre-storm prep
  • Planning to sell soon (may not recoup full cost)
