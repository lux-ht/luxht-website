Hurricane Preparedness: Protecting Your Florida Home

Updated January 2026 • 10 min read

Living in Florida means accepting that hurricanes are part of life. The difference between minor inconvenience and major disaster often comes down to preparation. Here's your complete guide to protecting your home and family.

Understanding Florida Hurricane Season

Hurricane season officially runs from June 1 to November 30, with peak activity in August and September. Central Florida, while not directly on the coast, regularly experiences:

  • Hurricane-force winds (74+ mph)
  • Tropical storm conditions
  • Flooding from heavy rainfall
  • Tornado activity from outer bands
  • Extended power outages

Structural Preparations (Do Now, Not Later)

Roof Inspection and Reinforcement

Your roof is your first line of defense:

  • Annual inspection: Check for loose or missing shingles
  • Hurricane straps: Metal connectors that tie roof to walls ($1,500-$3,000 to add)
  • Sealed roof deck: Prevents water intrusion if shingles blow off
  • Impact-resistant shingles: Consider during next roof replacement

Window and Door Protection

  • Impact windows: Best protection, also reduces insurance ($15,000-$30,000)
  • Hurricane shutters: Accordion, roll-down, or panel types ($2,000-$10,000)
  • Plywood: Budget option, cut and label before season starts
  • Garage doors: Weakest point in many homes - reinforce or replace
  • Entry doors: Ensure proper deadbolts and reinforced frames

Landscaping Safety

  • Trim dead branches and weak limbs
  • Remove trees close to structures if diseased
  • Choose wind-resistant plants for new landscaping
  • Secure or store outdoor furniture and decorations

Insurance Discounts

Many hurricane-resistant upgrades qualify for insurance discounts. Impact windows, hurricane shutters, and roof upgrades can reduce premiums by 15-45%. Get a wind mitigation inspection to document improvements.

Before a Storm (72-48 Hours Out)

  • Review your evacuation zone and routes
  • Fill vehicles with gas
  • Withdraw cash (ATMs may not work)
  • Fill prescriptions
  • Charge all devices and backup batteries
  • Fill bathtubs with water for flushing toilets
  • Document belongings with photos for insurance
  • Locate important documents

24 Hours Before

  • Install shutters or plywood
  • Bring in all outdoor items
  • Turn refrigerator and freezer to coldest setting
  • Fill coolers with ice
  • Park vehicles in garage or away from trees
  • Turn off propane tanks
  • Unplug electronics to prevent surge damage

Essential Supply Checklist

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day (7-day supply)
  • Non-perishable food (7-day supply)
  • Manual can opener
  • Flashlights and batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Medications (2-week supply)
  • Cash in small bills
  • Important documents in waterproof container
  • Phone chargers and power banks
  • Radio (battery or hand-crank)

After the Storm

Returning Home Safely

  • Wait for official all-clear
  • Watch for downed power lines
  • Don't wade in floodwater
  • Check for gas leaks before entering
  • Document all damage before cleanup
  • Take photos and video for insurance

Common Post-Storm Repairs

  • Roof tarping and repair
  • Water extraction and drying
  • Window and door replacement
  • Tree removal
  • Fence repair
  • Pool cage repair or replacement
  • Mold remediation

Long-Term Hurricane-Resistant Upgrades

Consider these improvements during your next renovation:

  1. Impact-resistant windows and doors - Best ROI for protection and insurance savings
  2. Metal roof or impact-rated shingles - Longer-lasting storm protection
  3. Whole-home generator - Comfort during extended outages
  4. Reinforced garage door - Critical weak point
  5. Secondary water resistance - Sealed roof deck
