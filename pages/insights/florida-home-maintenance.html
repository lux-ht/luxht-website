Florida Home Maintenance Calendar

Updated January 2026 • 10 min read

Florida's unique climate demands a different maintenance schedule than homes in other states. High humidity, intense sun, heavy rains, and hurricane season all impact your home differently. Here's your complete year-round guide.

January - February: Post-Holiday Checkup

Interior Tasks

  • Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
  • Check water heater for sediment buildup
  • Inspect caulking around tubs, showers, sinks
  • Check under sinks for leaks
  • Clean refrigerator coils
  • Test garage door auto-reverse safety

Exterior Tasks

  • Inspect roof for damage from winter storms
  • Check for any foundation cracks
  • Trim dead branches from trees
  • Service irrigation system
  • Pressure wash exterior (before pollen season)

March - April: Spring Prep & AC Season

HVAC Preparation

  • Schedule AC tune-up (critical for Florida!)
  • Replace air filters
  • Clean condensate drain line with vinegar
  • Clear debris from outdoor unit
  • Check thermostat batteries

Exterior Maintenance

  • Inspect and clean gutters
  • Check roof for winter damage
  • Touch up exterior paint where peeling
  • Re-caulk windows and doors as needed
  • Seal driveway cracks before summer rains

Lawn and Landscape

  • Apply pre-emergent herbicide
  • Fertilize lawn (before summer stress)
  • Check irrigation heads and coverage
  • Trim trees and shrubs away from house

Florida AC Reality

Your AC will run 8-10 months per year in Florida. Annual professional maintenance in spring is not optional—it prevents breakdowns during the hottest months when repair companies are busiest.

May - Early June: Hurricane Prep Begins

Hurricane Preparation

  • Inventory and test hurricane shutters
  • Check emergency supplies and refresh as needed
  • Trim trees—remove dead limbs and weak branches
  • Clear garage for vehicle storage during storms
  • Document home contents for insurance
  • Review insurance policies

Exterior Check

  • Inspect roof before storm season
  • Check that gutters drain properly
  • Ensure grading directs water away from foundation
  • Inspect pool cage for weak spots

June - August: Rainy Season Vigilance

Monthly During Rainy Season

  • Check AC filter (heavy use = faster clogging)
  • Inspect attic for leaks after major storms
  • Monitor indoor humidity (should stay below 60%)
  • Check for standing water around foundation
  • Watch for signs of mold or mildew

Ongoing Tasks

  • Keep gutters clear of debris
  • Adjust irrigation for increased rainfall
  • Treat for pests (they're active in warm, wet conditions)
  • Monitor pool chemistry closely

September - October: Post-Hurricane Season

After Storm Season

  • Thorough roof inspection
  • Check for water intrusion damage
  • Inspect and store hurricane shutters
  • Inventory and rotate emergency supplies

Fall Maintenance

  • Clean and store pool cage screens if removable
  • Pressure wash exterior after storm season
  • Check exterior paint for storm damage
  • Service pool equipment

November - December: Mild Weather Projects

Perfect Time for Projects

  • Exterior painting (mild temps, low humidity)
  • Driveway sealing
  • Deck staining or sealing
  • Landscaping projects

Year-End Checkup

  • Inspect and clean dryer vent
  • Test all smoke/CO detectors
  • Check water heater temperature (120°F recommended)
  • Flush water heater to remove sediment
  • Check weather stripping on doors

Monthly Florida Tasks

  • Replace or check AC filter
  • Test garage door safety features
  • Check for pest activity
  • Inspect irrigation system
  • Monitor humidity levels

Quarterly Tasks

  • Clean gutters and downspouts
  • Test water pressure
  • Check for toilet leaks (dye test)
  • Inspect caulking around tubs and windows
  • Clean range hood filter
