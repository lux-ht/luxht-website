7 Things to Check Before Buying a Home

Updated January 2026 • 7 min read

Buying a home is likely the largest investment you'll ever make. Before falling in love with granite countertops and staged furniture, it's crucial to look beyond the surface. Here's what experienced renovation professionals check when evaluating a property.

1. Foundation and Structural Integrity

The foundation is literally what your investment rests upon. Look for these warning signs:

  • Cracks in foundation walls - Hairline cracks are often cosmetic, but horizontal cracks or those wider than 1/4" warrant professional evaluation
  • Uneven floors - Bring a marble or ball and see if it rolls
  • Doors and windows that stick - Could indicate settling or shifting
  • Gaps between walls and ceiling/floor
  • Bowing or leaning walls

Cost to repair: Foundation issues can range from $5,000 for minor repairs to $50,000+ for major structural work.

2. Roof Condition and Age

A roof replacement is one of the most expensive repairs a homeowner faces. Check:

  • Age: Asphalt shingles last 20-25 years; tile can last 50+
  • Missing, curling, or damaged shingles
  • Sagging sections - Could indicate structural damage or water damage
  • Flashing condition around chimneys, vents, skylights
  • Signs of leaks in attic - Stains, mold, daylight visible

Cost to repair: Minor repairs $500-$2,000; full replacement $8,000-$25,000+

3. Electrical System

Outdated electrical can be a safety hazard and expensive to upgrade:

  • Panel capacity: Modern homes need 200-amp service minimum
  • Wiring type: Aluminum wiring (1960s-70s) requires special attention
  • Knob and tube wiring: Found in pre-1950s homes, often uninsurable
  • GFCI outlets in kitchens, bathrooms, outdoors
  • Number of outlets: Older homes may need additional circuits

Cost to repair: Panel upgrade $1,500-$3,000; complete rewire $10,000-$30,000

4. Plumbing System

Hidden plumbing problems can lead to major water damage:

  • Pipe material: Galvanized steel pipes corrode and need replacement
  • Polybutylene pipes: Common in 1980s-90s, prone to failure
  • Water pressure: Should be 40-80 PSI
  • Water heater age: Tank heaters last 8-12 years
  • Signs of leaks: Water stains, musty smells, warped flooring
  • Sewer line condition: Request a sewer scope inspection for older homes

Cost to repair: Pipe repairs $500-$5,000; complete re-pipe $4,000-$15,000

5. HVAC System

In Florida's climate, a properly functioning AC is essential:

  • Age: AC units typically last 10-15 years
  • Efficiency rating: Look for SEER 14 or higher
  • Ductwork condition: Leaky ducts waste 25-40% of conditioned air
  • Recent service records: Annual maintenance is essential
  • Even cooling throughout home

Cost to repair: AC replacement $5,000-$12,000; ductwork $2,000-$6,000

Pro Tip: Get Multiple Inspections

A standard home inspection covers general conditions, but consider specialized inspections for: termites/WDO, sewer lines, pool/spa, roof, and mold. Each costs $100-$500 but can save you thousands.

6. Water Damage and Mold

Florida's humidity makes mold a serious concern:

  • Water stains on ceilings, walls, or around windows
  • Musty odors especially in closets, bathrooms, basements
  • Bubbling or peeling paint
  • Warped or soft flooring
  • Previous flood history - Check FEMA flood maps
  • Visible mold in bathrooms, under sinks, in attic

Cost to repair: Minor mold remediation $500-$3,000; major $10,000-$30,000+

7. Permits and Unpermitted Work

Unpermitted work can become your problem after purchase:

  • Request permit history from the local building department
  • Compare to seller disclosures - All renovations should be listed
  • Look for obvious additions like enclosed garages, bonus rooms, pools
  • Verify pool permits - Unpermitted pools can be costly to legalize

Risk: Unpermitted work may need to be brought to code or removed; can affect insurance and resale.

Questions to Ask the Seller

  1. How old is the roof, HVAC, and water heater?
  2. Have there been any insurance claims on the property?
  3. Are there any known defects or issues?
  4. What renovations or improvements have been made?
  5. Were permits pulled for all work?
  6. How long have you owned the home?
  7. Why are you selling?
  8. What are the average utility costs?
