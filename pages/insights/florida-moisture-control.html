Moisture Control in Florida Homes

Updated January 2026 • 8 min read

With average humidity levels of 70-90%, Florida presents unique challenges for homeowners. Uncontrolled moisture leads to mold, structural damage, and health problems. Here's how to keep your home dry and healthy.

Understanding Florida's Humidity Challenge

Florida's subtropical climate creates a constant battle against moisture:

  • Average annual humidity: 74.5%
  • Summer humidity: Often 80-90%
  • Rainy season: June through September
  • Average annual rainfall: 50+ inches

This constant moisture creates ideal conditions for mold growth, wood rot, and insect infestations.

Signs of Moisture Problems

  • Musty or moldy odors
  • Condensation on windows
  • Peeling paint or wallpaper
  • Warped or buckling wood floors
  • Visible mold spots (black, green, or white)
  • Increased allergy symptoms
  • Sticking doors and windows
  • Rust on metal surfaces
  • Damp or wet crawl spaces

Indoor Humidity Control

Ideal Indoor Humidity: 45-55%

Keep humidity in this range to prevent mold growth while maintaining comfort.

Air Conditioning

Your AC is your primary dehumidifier:

  • Properly sized system is crucial - oversized units cool too fast without removing humidity
  • Keep thermostat at 78°F or lower during humid months
  • Use "auto" fan setting, not "on" - continuous fan reintroduces moisture
  • Change filters monthly during high-use periods
  • Annual maintenance keeps system running efficiently

Whole-Home Dehumidifiers

For persistent humidity problems:

  • Integrates with HVAC system
  • Removes 70-150 pints of water daily
  • Cost: $1,500-$3,000 installed
  • Reduces AC workload and energy costs

Portable Dehumidifiers

Good for problem areas:

  • Closets, laundry rooms, garages
  • Empty water reservoir daily or use continuous drain
  • Clean filter regularly

Vacation Mode Warning

Never turn off AC completely when away. Set to 80°F maximum. Florida homes can develop mold within 48-72 hours in hot, humid conditions with no air circulation.

Ventilation Essentials

Bathroom Ventilation

  • Run exhaust fans during and 30 minutes after showers
  • Ensure fans vent outside, not into attic
  • Consider humidity-sensing fans that run automatically
  • Clean fan covers regularly

Kitchen Ventilation

  • Use range hood when cooking
  • Ensure it vents outside
  • Recirculating hoods don't remove moisture

Dryer Ventilation

  • Ensure dryer vents outside
  • Clean vent regularly (fire hazard and efficiency)
  • Consider heat pump dryers for even less moisture

Exterior Moisture Prevention

Grading and Drainage

  • Ground should slope away from foundation (6" drop over 10 feet)
  • Clean gutters and downspouts regularly
  • Extend downspouts at least 4 feet from foundation
  • Install French drains if water pools near home

Landscaping

  • Avoid planting shrubs directly against house
  • Trim vegetation for air circulation
  • Use mulch sparingly near foundation
  • Ensure sprinklers don't spray house

Exterior Maintenance

  • Seal cracks in stucco and exterior walls
  • Maintain caulking around windows and doors
  • Repair roof leaks immediately
  • Check flashing around chimneys and vents

Mold Prevention and Treatment

Prevention

  • Keep humidity below 60%
  • Fix leaks within 24-48 hours
  • Ensure proper ventilation
  • Use mold-resistant products in renovations
  • Regular cleaning of prone areas

When to Call Professionals

  • Mold covers area larger than 10 square feet
  • Mold in HVAC system
  • After flooding or major water damage
  • Health symptoms persist
  • Mold returns after cleaning

Florida-Specific Building Practices

When renovating, ensure contractors use Florida-appropriate methods:

  • Vapor barriers: Placed on exterior (opposite of northern states)
  • Mold-resistant drywall: In bathrooms and high-moisture areas
  • Pressure-treated lumber: For any ground contact
  • Proper HVAC sizing: Manual J calculation essential
  • Sealed ductwork: Prevents moisture in attic from entering
